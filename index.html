About Me

I am a senior undergraduate student from Dept. of Computer Science at Tsinghua University. My research interest focuses primarily on data mining, especially its combination with economics, linguistics and social sciences. Meanwhile, I am interested in machine learning, natural language processing and information retrieval.

Currently, my advisors are Prof. Jie Tang at Tsinghua University and Prof. Philip S. Yu at University of Illinois at Chicago.

  • FPGA Challenge Project

    Tsinghua U, Beijing    Sep-Dec 2014

    We used reinforcement learning to optimize inference algorithms. By learning where to sample, we could speed up the run-time performance 2X-5X for a wide range of models. Learn more

    The following plot visualizes the behavior of the optimized sampler. This is a Name Entitiy Recognition task. Each row is a snapshot of the number of samples generated several steps. Darker cells mean more cumulative samples generated.
    Without optimization, the sampler (cyclic Gibbs) allocates computational resources uniformly. While the optimized (heterogeneous) sampler knows where to sample.
