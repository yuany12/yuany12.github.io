M-square: An Opinion Spam Detection Framework using Multi-site Temporal Sentimental Patterns

Yuan Yuan, Sihong Xie, Chun-ta Lu, Jie Tang and Philip S. Yu

WWW 2016: Submitted to 25th International Conference on World Wide Web

Abstract: Online review websites have been serving as hubs of millions of opinionated texts and ratings for businesses worldwide. The ratings and sentiments on these websites can significantly and directly influence businesses and customer experiences. Meanwhile, underground and organized spammers are creating a huge volume of fake reviews to hijack the review systems to oversell or defame businesses purposely. It is important to uncover these spamming attacks and eliminate unjust ratings and reviews to protect all participants in the systems. Inspired by the comprehensive and complementary information contained in multiple data sources, we argue that signals aggregated from multiple review websites can provide e↵ective and human-interpretable evidences than those from any single website. Previous studies either fail to jointly model rating and review data from multiple sites, or lack of a principled and streamlined spam detection pipeline to model and exploit multiple sources. We close this gap by proposing a two-level detection pipeline. We first design a suite of cross-site sentimental time series patterns that are indicative of high level abnormal activities. We then characterize these patterns and identify the most predictive ones for spam detection. The patterns and a general and scalable pattern detection algorithm comprise the macroscopic step. We demonstrate via human annotations, that the pipeline can spot a sizable collection of spams that have filtrated one of the current commercial anti-spam systems. To further identify individual these un-spotted spams on the microscopic level, we study and identify various textual and behavioral features that are correlated with the spams within the detected time windows. The overall pipeline is simple, effective and scalable, and complements the current state-of-the-art approaches.
